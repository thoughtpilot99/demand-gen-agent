1 What you are standing up

A teammate that runs paid, not another dashboard you have to check.

Most paid teams find out a campaign went sideways days after the money was gone. This one watches the spend as it happens, acts inside your guardrails, and pings you when something needs your eyes. Four things it does:

Runs on Claude

Wired to your LinkedIn, Meta, Google, Reddit and X ad accounts through MetadataONE's MCP. One connection, all five channels.

Manages spend day to day

Rebids the auctions worth winning, pauses the losers, and moves budget toward whatever is returning pipeline.

Lives in your Slack

You run paid by messaging it. The big moves come back as an Approve or Hold card you sign off on in a thread.

Alerts and a live dashboard

Pushes alerts and a weekly readout into Slack, with a live dashboard for spend, CPL, and pipeline by channel.

2 How it works

It reads through one MCP and acts through your guardrails.

There is no magic here, and you can read every line of it. The agent reads your live numbers straight from MetadataONE. When it wants to change spend, it does not just do it. It runs the move through guardrails you set, and the big ones wait for you in Slack.

Read

Pulls live spend, CPL and pipeline by channel from MetadataONE's MCP.

Decide

Runs on Claude. Spots the channel bleeding money and the one returning pipeline.

Act

Rebids and pauses on its own. Routes big budget moves to you first.

Report

Posts what it did, and why, into Slack. Updates the dashboard.

The part that makes it safe

Reads run through Claude's MCP connector. Writes do not. Every change to spend goes through three guarded tools the agent has to call, where your auto-approve limit and the Slack sign-off are enforced in code. The agent proposes. Your rules decide.

3 Stand up your own

Five minutes, mostly pasting keys.

You need three things: a Claude API key, a MetadataONE access token, and a Slack app. Then it is one command.

1

Clone it and install

Grab the repo and pull the dependencies. Node 20 or newer.

terminalgit clone https://github.com/thoughtpilot99/demand-gen-agent cd demand-gen-agent npm install cp .env.example .env
2

Get your three keys

  • Claude. An API key from console.anthropic.com. Paste it as ANTHROPIC_API_KEY.
  • MetadataONE. Log into Metadata as an admin, open Settings, click Access Token, generate one for the MCP server, and paste it as METADATAONE_TOKEN.
  • Slack. Create an app at api.slack.com/apps with Socket Mode on. Drop in the bot token, app token, and signing secret.
3

Point it at your tools

MetadataONE exposes the write actions as MCP tools. Their names vary by tenant, so list yours and set them in .env.

terminalnpm run probe

That prints every tool your tenant exposes. Match the pause, bid, budget and performance tools to MCP_TOOL_PAUSE, MCP_TOOL_SET_BID, MCP_TOOL_MOVE_BUDGET and MCP_TOOL_GET_PERFORMANCE.

4

Set your guardrails

This is the part you actually tune. Decide what the agent does on its own versus what waits for you.

.env# Budget moves at or under this run on their own. AUTO_APPROVE_DAILY_USD=2000 # It will never propose a single move above this. MAX_DAILY_SHIFT_USD=20000 # When a channel crosses this CPL, the guard kicks in. CPL_CEILING_USD=140
5

Bring it online

One command. It comes up in Slack and starts watching.

terminalnpm run dev

Now go to your #paid-media channel and message it. Ask it how you are pacing. From here, you stop opening ad managers.

Optional, but nice

The live dashboard ships in the same repo under site/. Deploy it to Vercel in one push and you have a shareable link for spend, CPL and pipeline by channel, separate from the chat.

4 What a day looks like

It catches the spike intraday, not at month-end.

Meta CPL starts climbing at 11am because a creative fatigued. The agent sees it the moment it crosses your ceiling. It pauses the two weak ad sets, rebids your top Google auctions to hold the blended number, and shifts that spend toward LinkedIn where pipeline per dollar is highest. The small moves it just makes. The bigger budget reallocation it sends to you.

The guardrails, in one line each
  • Rebids and pauses are day-to-day work. They run automatically and the agent tells you what it did.
  • Budget moves at or under your limit run automatically too.
  • Budget moves above your limit post to Slack and wait for a human to approve in the thread.
  • Nothing above the hard ceiling. Ever. The agent breaks it into steps instead.
5 Talk to it like a teammate

Plain English. No syntax to learn.

You do not configure it with a form. You tell it what you want the way you would tell a colleague who runs paid. A few that work on day one:

slackhow are we pacing today? anything I should look at?
slackshift $3k/day from Meta to LinkedIn if LinkedIn is still returning better pipeline
slackwhat's my worst CPL channel right now, and what would you do about it?
slackpause anything over $150 CPL and tell me what you pulled

It reads your live numbers before it answers, so it is talking about your accounts, not guessing. When a move is bigger than your limit, it tells you it has sent it to Slack for sign-off. It does not pretend it is already done.

Before you run it on real spend

The approval queue is in memory so the giveaway stays a single file to run. Back it with Redis or a table before production so a restart never drops a pending move. It is one swap, called out in the code.
